In the past...

Effigies are animal shaped mounds built for many reasons, one reason is for burial as a way for Native Americans to give respect to their dead. Among the dead, artifacts such as ceramics would be buried. It is built as mounds of earth in the shapes of birds, bears, and water spirits along with a host of other various animals. The effigy mounds range extends from Dubuque, Iowa into southeast Minnesota, across southern Wisconsin from the Mississippi to Lake Michigan, with a vast majority of the mounds located in the south-central region of Wisconsin. Effigy mounds are a regional geographic and cultural phenomenon unique to southern Wisconsin. There were once approximately 20,000 mounds in Wisconsin, but know fewer than 4,000 remain.

In the present...

Many effigy mounds are destroyed due to ignorance, self interest gain like mining and farming and roads, natural causes such as changing waterbodies, or lost to history. Many states preserve and document their effigy mounds, such as Iowa with Marching Bears Mound Group and Ohio with Great Serpent Mound however compared to them Wisconsin is behind – in regard of protection and interpretation.
